About

I am an Associate Professor at the School of Computer Science and Technology, Soochow University. I also serve as a Senior Technical Consultant (Part-time) at Microsoft (Asia), China.

My research interests focus on Multimodal Computing (especially for Multimodal Information Extraction, Visual-Language Understanding and Generation, Embodied Intelligence), Affective Computing, Large Language Models and AI for Medical Diagnosis.

I received my Ph.D. degree from Soochow University in 2019, advised by Prof. Guodong Zhou and Prof. Shoushan Li.

Table-Critic: A Multi-Agent Framework for Collaborative Criticism and Refinement in Table Reasoning

Peiying Yu, Guoxing Chen, Jingjing Wang

A novel multi-agent framework designed to enhance structured table reasoning through collaborative error detection and refinement, demonstrating how structured collaboration among LLM-based agents can overcome inherent limitations in complex reasoning tasks.

CCF A AAAI

SQLFixAgent: Towards Semantic-Accurate Text-to-SQL Parsing via Consistency-Enhanced Multi-Agent Collaboration

Jipeng Cen, Jiaxin Liu, Zhixu Li, Jingjing Wang

A novel multi-agent collaborative framework designed to detect and repair erroneous SQL queries, achieving a 3% improvement in execution accuracy on the challenging BIRD benchmark while maintaining high token efficiency.

Hawkeye: Discovering and Grounding Implicit Anomalous Sentiment in Recon-videos via Scene-enhanced Video Large Language Model

Jianing Zhao, Jingjing Wang, Yujie Jin, Jiamin Luo, Guodong Zhou

The first scene-enhanced video-language model designed for anomaly detection, integrating multimodal context to recognize subtle anomalies and pinpoint their temporal boundaries in untrimmed videos.
